# Acceptable Use Policy — Praxia

> **⚠️ TEMPLATE NOTICE**: Review with counsel before relying on this in
> commercial agreements.

**Effective date**: TO BE FINALIZED

You may not use Praxia (the OSS or any hosted Service) to:

## Prohibited content

- Generate, distribute, or store content that is illegal in your or our
  jurisdiction
- Create child sexual abuse material (CSAM), non-consensual sexual
  imagery, or content depicting violence intended to incite harm
- Defame, harass, or impersonate any person or organization
- Infringe intellectual property, trade secrets, or rights of publicity

## Prohibited operations

- Generate or distribute malware, ransomware, viruses, or exploit code
- Conduct unauthorized security testing of third-party systems
- Mass surveillance, scraping, or unauthorized data collection
- Spam, phishing, or other deceptive electronic communications
- Build systems that bypass safety measures of third-party LLM providers

## Prohibited professional decisions

The bundled business-domain skills (investment, sales, design,
purchasing, patent, legal) include built-in guardrails reminding users
that **final professional advice requires a qualified human
professional**. You agree not to:

- Bypass or disable these guardrails
- Use Praxia output as final legal, medical, financial, tax, or
  regulatory advice without qualified human review
- Represent Praxia output as having been produced or verified by a
  qualified professional

## Prohibited platform abuse

- Disrupt, reverse-engineer, or compromise the Service or its
  infrastructure
- Probe, scan, or test for vulnerabilities outside of the responsible
  disclosure process described in [SECURITY.md](../../SECURITY.md)
- Use credentials, API keys, or accounts you are not authorized to use
- Violate rate limits, quotas, or fair-use guidelines

## Reporting violations

To report a violation, contact `security@praxia.dev` or
`abuse@praxia.dev`.

## Enforcement

We may suspend or terminate access to the Service for any violation,
with or without notice. Repeated or serious violations may be reported
to law enforcement.
